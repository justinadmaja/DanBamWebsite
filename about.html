The First DanBam

2006

DanBam first opened in the southern part of Seoul, South Korea by Mr. Jong Un from an abandoned barbershop. Combining traditional Korean recipes with a modern twist. DanBam became one of the most popular food stops in Seoul.

Seoul Expansion

2009

DanBam opened 2 more locations in Seoul due to DanBam's popularity and over crowdness in its old location. Pictured is one of DanBam's new location in Jongno District.

In this year, DanBam also introduced its first new menu release, adding several items such as Japchae, Korean-style Fried Rice and several Korean street foods, such as Bungeoppang (Korean Taiyaki) and Kimbap (Seaweed Rolls).

DanBam Breakthrough in Busan

2011

After DanBam's massive popularity in Seoul, Mr. Jong Un decided to expand DanBam to South Korea's second largest city, Busan.

The new Bulgogi recipe is also introduced in this year, after several inputs and suggestions from customers. Now, it is the most popular dish from DanBam.

DanBam Goes International

2014

After opening 3 more restaurants across South Korea, DanBam opened its first international branch in Tokyo, Japan.

To celebrate this momentuous achievement, DanBam released a special edition Korean Sushi dish to all DanBam restaurants for a year, consisting of Bulgogi meat and Kimchi toppings.

DanBam Goes to Taiwan

2017

DanBam opened its second international branch in Taipei City, Taiwan after the suprising success at Japan.

Just like Japan's international opening, DanBam also released a special Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup, consisting of slowly braised beef with a tangle of slurpable noodles with the addition of pickled mustard greens and the signature Five-Spice Powder

DanBam Menu Refresh

2020

Mr. Jong Un felt that it's time to refresh its menu. DanBam released its second menu in 2020, adding new dishes and introducing new recipes, such as the Bibimbap Rice Bowl, while removing some of the less popular dishes -such as the Korean street foods- to reduce menu clutter.
